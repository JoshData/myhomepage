Joshua Tauberer,  Ph.D.

A civic hacker* in Washington, DC, USA.

In 2004 I launched GovTrack.us, a government transparency tool for the U.S. Congress, and I have been tracking Congress ever since.

My part-time/consulting work currently includes LARSA, Inc. (a structural engineering software company) and the D.C. Council Office of the General Counsel.

My book Open Government Data: The Book (2012) discusses the principles behind the open government data movement in the United States, including uses for transparency and civic engagement, a brief legal history, data quality, civic hacking, and paradoxes in transparency.

I also co-organize Open Data Day DC each year, support other hackathons, and am a member of Code for DC.

*Civic hacking is creative use of technology to innovate our civic lives, especially our participation in government.
